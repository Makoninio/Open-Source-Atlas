note 01

Popularity is the easiest part to measure.

Stars are visible because they are light. Maintenance is harder to see because it lives in repeated labor: triage, review, compatibility fixes, and long threads that never make it into a tagline.

note 02

Older projects inherit their own history.

A mature repository is not just bigger. It is burdened by past decisions, user expectations, old interfaces, and the cost of staying dependable.

note 03

Open source still comes down to specific people.

Even when a project looks communal from far away, contribution histories often collapse into a smaller circle of maintainers doing the slow, repetitive work.

REPOSITORY SNAPSHOT

These repositories do not tell the same story.

Some are old and stable. Some are huge and noisy. Some look calm until you read the maintenance burden hiding underneath. The cards are meant to be read comparatively, not just individually.

TIMELINE

Repository age changes the kind of pressure a project carries.

Newer projects often grow in crowded ecosystems and move quickly. Older ones have had time to become standards, but they also have more compatibility debt and more history to support.

COMMIT MOMENTUM

Development is rarely steady for long.

Release cycles, migrations, bug cleanups, and ecosystem changes create bursts. A quiet line can mean stability, or neglect, or that the hard work moved somewhere less visible.

MAINTAINERS

Eventually the story stops being about repositories and starts being about people.

Open source gets described like infrastructure, but its continuity still depends on recurring human decisions: who reviews, who fixes, who answers, who stays.
